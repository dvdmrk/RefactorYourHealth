Before We Start...

Nutrition

It's 30% fitness, 70% nutrition.

  • Change mostly comes from the kitchen.
  • The KITCHEN, not the BAG.
  • Really, it's all about moderation.

DO's and DON'Ts

DO

  • Cook as much as possible
  • Count your macros along with calories
  • Whole foods (not the company)
  • Snacks!
    • Nuts
    • Berries
    • Fruits
    • Hard Boiled Eggs
  • When in doubt while you're out, sort it out

DON'T

  • Carbonated drinks
  • Alcohol... I know what you're thinking.
  • Sauteed or Fried Foods
  • Fast Food

Exercise

Where do I start?

  • Starting is one of the hardest things to do; but easily the best way to be ahead of everyone else who has not.
  • Most folks search for a plan online
  • Most folks with money and time will get a gym membership
  • Most folks with more money and time hire a personal trainer
  • Many will make mistakes in the beginning

Audience Participation Time!

Recommendations

  • Ask a friend in the know
  • Hire a personal trainer AFTER doing your research
  • Find a local boot camp
  • Meetup!

Lifestyle

Sample demographics

  • Most people in tech have an 8-to-5, or 8 hour, workday which is... well, kinda nice.
  • Many would benefit from a 5-to-9 day (5 a.m. to 9 p.m.)
  • Most humans get less than 6.1 hours of sleep

Recommendations

  • You don't have to go to a gym to be active
    • Walking/jogging/running outside is fine!
    • A set of dumbbells and some floor space makes for an instant workout space
  • Have pets or children? They can participate too!
    • Children can join in on simple, non-resistance workouts
    • Pets like to run around as it is
    • Both will sleep well at night. Really.

Technology

Smart devices are everywhere

  • Nearly 5 billion people have a smart device
  • Half of them are smartphones
  • 167 million people are projected to have a wearable device by 2022
  • Everyone in this room has a smart phone, maybe a smart watch or tablet... or both

Fitness Tech

  • Every major company out there has some kind of fitness tracking or digital wellbeing application
    • Apple: HealthKit and Screen Time
    • Google: Google Fit and Digital Wellbeing
    • Under Armour: MapMyFitness and UA Record
    • Microsoft: ...uh...

Gamification

  • Smartphone Apps
    • Pokemon Go managed to get 750+ million people to go outside and walk with a pikachu and hatch their various "pokemon eggs".
    • Charity Miles has given nearly $3 million from its userbase of around 100K active users daily, many of them being runners
    • Zombies Run and Wizards Unite are also some honorable mentions
  • Arcades
    • Dance Dance Revolution is an arcade rhythm game with thousands of players around the world that uses foot-eye coordination for a fully active experience; the average 7-minute session burning 30-140 kcals.
